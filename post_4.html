Best Cashback Apps for Groceries in 2025

Cashback apps are one of the easiest ways to save money on groceries — you shop like normal, scan your receipt or link your loyalty card, and get real cash back. But not all apps are created equal. Some pay more, some work at more stores, and some can be stacked together for double the savings. Here's an honest ranking of the best ones in 2025.

💡 The stacking strategy: Most cashback apps can be used simultaneously on the same purchase. Buy an item on sale, clip an Ibotta offer, and scan with Fetch Rewards — you get all three discounts on the same transaction.

1

Ibotta — Best Overall

💰 Avg $240/year Works at 300+ stores Free

Ibotta is the gold standard of grocery cashback apps and consistently pays the most. You browse available offers before shopping, buy the qualifying products, then scan your receipt to claim cash back. Payouts go directly to PayPal, Venmo, or as gift cards.

What sets Ibotta apart is the sheer number of offers — hundreds of products across every category — and the fact that it works at over 300 retailers including Walmart, Target, Kroger, Costco, and most major chains. New users also get a welcome bonus just for signing up and completing their first redemption.

✅ Pros

  • Highest cash back of any app
  • Works at 300+ stores
  • Cash out via PayPal or Venmo
  • Welcome bonus for new users

❌ Cons

  • Must clip offers before shopping
  • Some offers are brand-specific
  • $20 minimum to cash out
2

Fetch Rewards — Easiest to Use

💰 $50–100/year Any receipt Free

Fetch Rewards wins on simplicity — you just scan any grocery receipt and earn points, no offer clipping required. Every receipt earns something, and certain brands earn bonus points. Points redeem for gift cards to Amazon, Target, Walmart, and more.

It pays less per transaction than Ibotta, but the zero-effort approach means you'll actually use it consistently. It's the perfect app to run alongside Ibotta — scan every receipt with both for maximum return.

✅ Pros

  • Scan any receipt — no pre-clipping
  • Works at any store
  • Very easy to use daily

❌ Cons

  • Lower value than Ibotta
  • Points not direct cash
  • Takes time to accumulate
3

Rakuten — Best for Online Orders

💰 Up to 10% back Online shopping Free

Rakuten (formerly Ebates) gives you a percentage of your purchase back when you shop through their portal or use their browser extension. For grocery delivery services like Instacart, Thrive Market, and HelloFresh, Rakuten frequently offers 3–10% cash back on your entire order.

This makes Rakuten especially powerful when combined with a delivery service subscription. Order through Rakuten → get the delivery service discount → earn cash back on top. Rakuten pays quarterly via PayPal or check.

✅ Pros

  • High % back on delivery services
  • Browser extension makes it automatic
  • Pays real cash (not points)

❌ Cons

  • Online orders only
  • Pays quarterly, not instantly
  • Rates change frequently
4

Checkout 51 — Best for Produce

💰 $100–150/year Fresh & produce offers Free

Checkout 51 stands out for having strong offers on fresh produce and generic items — categories that most cashback apps ignore in favor of brand-name packaged goods. If you buy a lot of fruits, vegetables, and store-brand products, Checkout 51 fills a gap that Ibotta doesn't cover as well.

Offers refresh every Thursday, so it's worth checking weekly. Cash out via check once you hit $20.

✅ Pros

  • Strong produce & generic offers
  • Works at any store
  • New offers every Thursday

❌ Cons

  • Fewer offers than Ibotta
  • Check payout only (slower)
  • $20 minimum cashout
5

Kroger App — Best Store Loyalty App

💰 $200+/year Kroger stores only Free

If you shop at Kroger (or any Kroger-owned store like Fred Meyer, King Soopers, or Ralphs), their digital coupon app is one of the highest-value loyalty programs in grocery retail. Digital coupons apply automatically at checkout when you scan your loyalty card — no paper clipping needed.

The fuel points program adds extra value — earn points on grocery purchases and redeem for significant discounts at the pump. Heavy Kroger shoppers can save well over $200/year between digital coupons and fuel savings combined.

✅ Pros

  • Automatic coupons at checkout
  • Fuel points add extra value
  • Personalized deals based on history

❌ Cons

  • Kroger stores only
  • Must clip digital coupons in app first
💡 Maximum savings stack: Use Ibotta for clipped offers + Fetch Rewards on every receipt + Rakuten when ordering delivery online. Running all three costs nothing and takes less than 5 minutes per shopping trip.

The Bottom Line

Start with Ibotta — it pays the most and is worth the effort of clipping offers. Add Fetch Rewards as your always-on backup since it works on every receipt with zero setup. If you order groceries online, install the Rakuten browser extension and you'll earn cash back automatically without thinking about it.

Together these three apps can realistically save you $300–400/year with minimal extra effort.
