Where to Buy the Cheapest Avocados in 2026

📊 Updated April 2026

Avocados are one of the most price-volatile items in any grocery store. The same fruit can cost $0.75 at one store and $2.79 at another — literally four times the price for the same avocado. If you eat avocados regularly, knowing where to buy them can save you a surprising amount of money over the course of a year. We compared prices across the major grocery chains so you don't have to guess.

💡 Good news for avocado lovers: Avocado prices dropped more than 19% in late 2025 compared to the year before, according to market data. 2026 is shaping up to be one of the more affordable years for avocados in recent memory — making now a great time to stock up.

Store by Store Breakdown

$0.75each

🏆 Aldi — The Clear Winner

Aldi sells large Hass avocados for just $0.75 each — the cheapest price of any major grocery chain in the US. If you buy four avocados a week, shopping at Aldi instead of Whole Foods saves you over $100 a year on avocados alone. The quality is consistently good and the size is comparable to what you'd find at more expensive stores. If there's an Aldi near you, this is where you should always buy your avocados.

$0.85each

Costco — Best for Bulk Buyers

Costco sells avocados in 6-packs for around $5.10, which works out to roughly $0.85 per avocado. That's very close to Aldi's price and a great deal — if you go through avocados quickly. The catch is you have to buy six at once, so this only works if you use them fast enough before they over-ripen. Buy them slightly firm and let them ripen at home over 2-3 days.

$0.89each

Target — Best Convenience Option

At $0.89 each, Target is only 14 cents more than Aldi per avocado. If you're already doing a Target run, it's absolutely worth picking up avocados there rather than making a separate trip to Aldi. The price difference over a year is minimal if you're already at Target anyway.

$1.25each

Walmart — Best for Non-Aldi Shoppers

Walmart's avocado prices vary between $0.98 and $1.25 depending on size and whether they're sold individually or in bags. Bags of 4-6 avocados are usually the better deal per unit. Not as cheap as Aldi but still reasonable — and you can get them delivered at in-store prices with a Walmart+ membership.

$1.29each

Trader Joe's — Popular but Pricier

Trader Joe's charges $1.29 per avocado — more than 70% more expensive than Aldi. The quality is good but there's no compelling reason to buy avocados here when cheaper options are available. Worth it only if Trader Joe's is your primary store and you're already there.

$2.79each

Whole Foods — Most Expensive, Skip It

Whole Foods charges $2.79 for an organic large Hass avocado — nearly four times what you'd pay at Aldi. Even on sale, you're paying significantly more than you need to. Unless you specifically want certified organic avocados and don't have access to other stores, Whole Foods is not the place to buy avocados.

How to Keep Avocados Fresh Longer

One of the biggest avocado money wasters is buying them and having them go bad before you use them. Here's how to manage ripeness like a pro:

🟢 Buy firm, ripen at home

Buy firm avocados and let them ripen on the counter for 2-4 days. This gives you more control over timing than buying pre-ripened ones.

🧊 Refrigerate to slow ripening

Once an avocado is ripe, put it in the fridge. It will stay good for 2-3 more days — buying you time to use it.

🍋 Lemon juice prevents browning

If you only use half, squeeze lemon or lime juice on the cut side and store with the pit in. The acid slows oxidation significantly.

❄️ Freeze for long-term storage

Mash ripe avocados with a little lemon juice and freeze in portions. Perfect for smoothies, guacamole, or avocado toast later.

💡 The bulk buying trick: Buy a 6-pack at Costco when firm. Use 2 immediately, leave 2 on the counter to ripen over the next 3 days, and refrigerate the last 2 while still firm. This spreads your avocado supply across nearly a full week from a single purchase.

The Bottom Line

Aldi wins on price — at $0.75 per avocado it's the cheapest option by a significant margin. If you have an Aldi nearby, make it your go-to for avocados.

Costco and Target are strong alternatives at $0.85 and $0.89 respectively — worth it for convenience if you're already shopping there.

Avoid Whole Foods for avocados unless you specifically need organic — at $2.79 each you're paying nearly four times the Aldi price for essentially the same fruit.

With avocado prices down 19% from last year, 2026 is actually a great time to enjoy them more often — just make sure you're buying from the right store.
